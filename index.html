The Gulch

1

What Lifts You — Angel Wings

302 11th Ave S, Nashville, TN 37203
The most iconic mural in all of Nashville. Kelsey Montague's intricate lace-patterned wings in white on black are unmistakable — and Nashville-specific, with guitars woven into the feathers. There's also a tiny set of baby wings tucked to the side for a silly second shot. Come early; there's often a line by 10 AM.
⏱ Can have a line 👯 Great for group

Candy Hearts Mural

601 8th Ave S, Nashville, TN 37203 (Finnley's Boutique)
This one was literally commissioned with bachelorette parties in mind. The giant candy hearts say things like "Bach Please" and "Engaged AF" — it's colorful, the wall is big enough for all 9 of you, and the boutique next door is worth a quick browse. An absolute must for the group photo of the trip.

Broadway

Country Legends Mural

428 Broadway, Nashville, TN 37203 (side of Legends Corner)
On the side wall of the legendary Legends Corner bar, artist Tim Davis painted portraits of the Mount Rushmore of country music: Dolly Parton, Loretta Lynn, Merle Haggard, Willie Nelson, Johnny Cash, and more. Street parking nearby. If the group wants to pop in for a quick drink, Legends Corner has no cover charge and live music starts at 10 AM.

Off The Wall Nashville

Before You Go

Tips for The Van Crew

⏰

Go Early

The Wings and I Believe in Nashville murals get lines by 10–11 AM on weekends. Hit the Gulch first at 9 AM and you'll walk right up.

🅿️

Parking the Transit

The Gulch has a paid lot on 11th Ave. 12 South has a lot directly across from the I Believe mural. Off The Wall and East Nashville are street parking — easy.

🗺️

Bonus Murals

Pull up the Salt Water Vibes Google Map for extras you find along the way — the city is full of them.

📸

Photo Order

Bring one person whose sole job is photographer at each stop. Designate before you leave — it makes each stop faster and the photos way better.

🍳

Fuel Up in 12 South

Five Daughters Bakery (donuts), Edley's BBQ, and Milk and Honey are all within the 12 South stretch. Build in 30–45 min for a bite.

💡

Lighting Tips

Morning light (9–11 AM) is best for The Gulch since the walls face east. 12 South gets great afternoon light. East Nashville pops around midday.

Bonus Tour A

Music Row & Nashville Icons

~1.5 hrs All free from the car Downtown loop
1

Walk of Fame Park

121 4th Ave S, Nashville, TN 37201
Start here. Bronze stars embedded in the sidewalk honor country legends — Dolly, Cash, Garth, Loretta, and dozens more. It's free, open, and right on the edge of the action. A short walk from parking on 4th Ave.
Maps
🚗 Drive ~5 min northwest up 4th Ave
2

Ryman Auditorium — The Mother Church

116 5th Ave N, Nashville, TN 37219
Even from the outside, this place has gravity. The original home of the Grand Ole Opry — built in 1892 as a church — is one of the most legendary music venues in the world. Drive past or park and photograph the stained glass from the sidewalk. Free to admire; self-guided tours available inside for a fee.
Maps
🚗 Drive ~5 min southwest on Division St
3

Music Row — The Actual Row

16th & 17th Ave S, Nashville, TN 37203
This is where country music is made. Drive slowly up 16th and 17th Ave South and you'll pass the studios, publishers, and labels responsible for basically every country hit since the 1950s. RCA Studio B — where Elvis, Dolly, Roy Orbison, and Chet Atkins recorded — is at 1611 Roy Acuff Place. The exterior is free to see any time.
Maps
🚗 Drive ~3 min, stay on Division St going west
4

Musica Roundabout Sculpture

Music Row Roundabout, Demonbreun St & 16th Ave, Nashville, TN 37212
Right at the center of the Music Row roundabout stands "Musica" — nine large bronze figures dancing and celebrating music. They are also completely naked. It's unexpected, impressive, and very Nashville. You'll drive right around it as you exit Music Row. You're welcome.
Maps

Stars' Homes Drive

Taylor Swift's Estate — Forest Hills

Forest Hills neighborhood, Nashville, TN 37215
Taylor's main Nashville estate is in the upscale Forest Hills neighborhood, about 10 minutes south of downtown. The compound is gated and heavily wooded — you won't see much — but the neighborhood itself is beautiful to drive through, and you can say you did it. Dolly Parton's Brentwood Home

Brentwood, TN 37027
Dolly's Tennessee home is in Brentwood, a beautifully manicured suburb just south of Nashville proper. It's private but the neighborhood is peaceful and gorgeous to drive through. Belle Meade — Celebrity Row

Belle Meade, Nashville, TN 37205
Belle Meade is Nashville's oldest and most exclusive enclave — the kind of neighborhood where Reese Witherspoon, Martina McBride, and a rotating cast of country royalty have called home. The estates are massive and many are actually visible from the road. Drive slowly down Belle Meade Blvd for the main strip of jaw-dropping homes.

Bonus Tour C — The Unhinged One

The WTF Nashville Tour

~1–1.5 hrs Chaotic energy Free & weird
1

The Parthenon — Full-Scale Ancient Greek Temple in Tennessee

2500 West End Ave, Nashville, TN 37203 (Centennial Park)
Nashville's most baffling landmark: a full-scale, archaeologically accurate replica of the Parthenon sitting in a city park. Built in 1897 for the Tennessee Centennial Exposition, it was supposed to be temporary — but nobody wanted to take it down. Inside is a 42-foot gold-plated statue of Athena, the tallest indoor sculpture in the Western world. The outside is completely free to see any time. Just pull up, stare, and question everything.
Maps
🚗 Drive ~5 min east back toward Music Row
2

The "Musica" Naked People Roundabout

Demonbreun St & 16th Ave S, Nashville, TN 37212
Right in the middle of the Music Row roundabout is a massive bronze sculpture of nine nude figures dancing and playing music. It's called "Musica," it's been there since 2003, and nobody who drives around it for the first time is prepared. You literally circle it in the car. Perfect for a dramatic slow drive-by with all windows down.
Maps
🚗 Drive ~8 min south on Nolensville Pike
3

The Giant Cowboy Boot Water Tower

Glencliff neighborhood, Nashville, TN 37211 (near Nolensville Pike & Harding Pl)
There is a water tower in South Nashville painted to look like a giant cowboy boot. That's it. That's the stop. It's enormous, it's visible from the road, and it's one of the most authentically Nashville things in existence. Technically it's officially called the "Glencliff Water Tower" but everyone just calls it the boot.
Maps
🚗 Drive ~12 min back north to Madison
4

Santa's Pub — Year-Round Christmas Karaoke Trailer

2225 Bransford Ave, Nashville, TN 37204
Santa's Pub is a double-wide trailer permanently decorated for Christmas, open year-round, serving cheap beer and hosting karaoke every single night. There is no sign. It is cash only. It is one of the most beloved dive bars in America, featured in countless "best bars" lists nationwide. You don't have to go in (but you absolutely should). Even just driving past and seeing the Christmas lights blazing in the middle of summer is peak Nashville weird.
